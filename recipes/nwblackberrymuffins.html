Northwest Blackberry Muffins

by Amity Warme


Description:

These blackberry muffins are an utter delight! Just catching a whiff while they bake will leave your mouth watering. As a bonus, this recipe swaps out half of the flour for protein powder and doesn't have any added sugar - so they're delicious and healthy! This recipe yields ten muffins.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup plant-based milk of choice + 1 Tbsp lemon juice OR white vinegar
  • 2 overripe bananas
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla
  • 2 1/2 cups flour (see notes for options)
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 scoops Enduro Bites Vanilla Recovery Protein Powder
  • 1 1/2 - 2 cups blackberries (fresh or frozen)

Instructions:

  1. Combine plant-based milk of choice and lemon juice or vinegar in a liquid measuring cup and let sit for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, mash bananas thoroughly. Add eggs, vanilla, and homemade buttermilk (from step 1) and stir to combine.
  3. Add flour, cinnamon, salt, baking powder, baking soda, and Vanilla Recovery Protein Powder to the bowl. Stir until just combined.
  4. Add in the blackberries!
  5. Cover bowl and place in the fridge for at least 1 hour and up to overnight.
  6. When you are ready to bake your muffins, preheat oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit and prepare a muffin tin by greasing each muffin cup with cooking spray.
  7. Remove batter from fridge. Batter will be thick, but try not to stir it as you scoop batter into each muffin cup. Fill to the top! Recipe should divide into 10 muffins.
  8. Sprinkle a dash of cinnamon on the top of each muffin.
  9. Bake at 425 degrees Fahrenheit for 8 minutes. Then, reduce heat to 350 degrees Fahrenheit and bake for another 12 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out cleanly.
  10. Remove the muffins from the oven and let cool in the pan on a cooling rack. Then remove from the pan (a plastic knife works wonders) and enjoy!

Notes:

  • Make it vegan by replacing eggs with flax eggs.
  • Flour options: whole wheat flour, white whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, fluten-free all-purpose blend, whole wheat pastry flour. These will all yield slightly different flavors and textures. Whole wheat will be more dense, all-purpose will be lighter and fluffier. White whole wheat will fall in between. I haven't experimented with gluten-free yet, but I imagine it would work just fine.
  • For a sweeter muffin, add a few tablespoons of honey or pure maple syrup to the batter.
